# Attack outside Network
- "Port forwarding"
- is a networking technique that lets external devices on the internet access a specific service running on a device inside a private local network (LAN).
- Normally, devices inside your home or company network use private IP addresses (like 192.168.x.x) that are not reachable directly from the internet. Your router has a public IP, and it blocks unsolicited incoming connections by default.
- Port forwarding creates an explicit rule in the router to allow certain incoming traffic through.
